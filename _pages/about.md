I'm a rising senior at the University of Vermont studying Computer Science, with a minor in Epidemiology.

I started college as a Microbiology major before realizing my real interest was in computing. My passion is at the intersection of software and health -- using data to understand patterns in how diseases move throughout populations and implications on population health. My current focus is computational epidemiology, in which I'm learning to apply statistical and machine learning methods to surveillance data to uncover patterns that might be otherwise missed. Outside of my academics, I've worked as a pharmacy technician at CVS for two years which has helped keep me grounded on the patient level of healthcare.

My goal is to pursue a master's degree in computational epidemiology, and I'm actively looking for opportunities to build experience in the intersection of public health and data science.

Most recently, I analyzed temporal and magnitude abnormalities in Vermont's 2025-26 RSV season using wastewater data from [Data.CDC.gov][cdc]. Read more about it [here][rsv-epidemiology].

Please feel free to reach out to my UVM email address, <jacob.pancoast@uvm.edu> or my personal email, <jacob.pancoast17@gmail.com> for any inquiries, or to connect.
